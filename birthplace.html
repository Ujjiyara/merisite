My Birthplace

I was born in Amritsar, the city where I took my first breath, and the same place where my grandmother took her last. It’s a city that holds our family's entire emotional spectrum — the place that gave us everything and quietly took it all back. Amritsar isn’t just bricks and roads; it’s memory and meaning. It’s the scent of langar in the air, the sound of temple bells at dawn, the chaos of bazaars, and the silence of goodbyes. It’s where I learned how deeply a place can love you — and how painfully it can let you go. It raised me with warmth and wonder, then shaped me with loss and longing. And no matter how far I go, I carry a version of it in my heart — always golden, always home.
